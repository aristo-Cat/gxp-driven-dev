## 4. System context and boundary

**System boundary under analysis**: in scope — the temperature sensors, acquisition gateway, server application, database, web UI, and the interfaces to the site eQMS and data historian. Out of scope — the refrigeration equipment of the chamber itself (the system only monitors/records, it does not control temperature), and the receiving eQMS/historian internals.

**Supported GxP business process (origin of severity)**: GMP cold storage of temperature-sensitive product at 2–8 °C; recorded temperature is a GxP record supporting batch-release decisions. An undetected excursion can lead to compromised product reaching patients.

## 5. Step 1 — Initial Risk Assessment + System Impact

### 5.1 GxP determination

Is the system GxP-relevant? **gxp-relevant**

The system creates and stores temperature records as the primary GxP record and provides excursion detection for a GMP-controlled storage process (impact on product quality and, indirectly, patient safety).

### 5.2 System impact

Overall impact on patient safety / product quality / data integrity: **high**

| Axis | Impact (H/M/L) | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Patient Safety (PS) | M | The system is a monitoring/detection layer, not a control; an undetected excursion can lead to degraded product reaching patients, but additional controls exist (chamber alarms, QA release). |
| Product Quality (PQ) | H | The recorded conditions directly evidence whether stored product remained within its quality-critical range. |
| Data Integrity (DI) | H | The system is the primary electronic GxP record of storage temperature, with audit trail and signed reports. |

### 5.3 Determination of the GAMP category (critical output)

**Assigned category**: 4

**Rationale**: A commercial off-the-shelf monitoring platform that is **configured** to this process (alarm thresholds, notification rules, report templates, interfaces to eQMS/historian, user roles) but not custom-coded. Per GAMP 5 §M4 this is a configured product (Cat 4), like a SCADA/BMS/LIMS configuration — not a non-configured standard product (Cat 3) and not bespoke software (Cat 5).

| Category | Criterion (GAMP 5 §M4) | Applies? |
|---|---|---|
| **Cat 1** | Infrastructure software (OS, DB engine, middleware). Qualified, not validated. | No |
| **Cat 3** | Standard non-configured product (used out-of-the-box). | No — meaningful configuration is required |
| **Cat 4** | Configured product (LIMS, SCADA, ERP, CDS, EDMS, BMS, configurable spreadsheets). | **Yes** |
| **Cat 5** | Custom / bespoke application. | No — no custom code developed |

### 5.4 Applicability of 21 CFR Part 11 / EU Annex 11

Does the system generate/store GxP electronic records or use electronic signatures? **true**

| Question | Yes/No | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Does it generate/store GxP electronic records as the primary source? | Yes | → activate preset URS-EREC |
